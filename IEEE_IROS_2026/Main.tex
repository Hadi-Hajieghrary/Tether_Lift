% ======================== ABSTRACT ==============================
\begin{abstract}
We present the Geometric Position and Attitude Control (GPAC) architecture for decentralized cooperative aerial transport via flexible cable suspensions. Each of $N$~quadrotors runs an identical four-layer hierarchical controller on $\SEthree \times (\Sph^2)^N$ with no knowledge of $N$ or $m_L$, no centralized coordinator, and no exchange of payload, cable, or adaptive-parameter states---only neighbor GPS positions (10\,Hz) for collision avoidance. A concurrent learning adaptive law enables each drone to independently estimate its payload share $\hat{\theta}_i \to m_L/N$ from local cable measurements---without persistent excitation---such that $\sum_i \hat{\theta}_i \cdot u \to m_L \cdot u$. A modular CBF safety filter enforces cable tautness, angle, tilt, and collision constraints with input-to-state safety (ISSf) guarantees, and we prove that CBF-induced force modifications preserve the almost-global exponential stability of the geometric attitude controller on~$\SOthree$. Validated in a high-fidelity Drake simulation with bead-chain cables, 15-state ESKF, and Dryden wind, the architecture achieves 22.9\,cm tracking RMSE at under 1\,MFLOP/s per agent.
\end{abstract}
